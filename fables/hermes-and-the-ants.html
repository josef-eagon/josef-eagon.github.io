Hermes and the Ants

A man watched from the shoreline as a ship was struck by lightning and the crew was tossed about in the waves. The man cried out, “How unjust the gods must be to punish whichever impious one was aboard that ship at the expense of the innocent!”

As it happened, the man stood near an anthill. Bitten by one, he trampled the swarm underfoot. Hermes appeared striking with his staff, “You refuse to endure the judgment of the gods, while you are such with the ants.”

— — — — —

oho! it is easy to mimic when acting without intention. be wary of passing judgment on that which you don’t understand.
