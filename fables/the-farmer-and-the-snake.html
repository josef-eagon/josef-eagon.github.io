The Farmer and the Snake

A Farmer, concerned by a growing number of rats, allowed a Snake to move in under the doorstep. The Snake grew healthy from the arrangement, and resolved to ignore the Farmer. One day the Farmer's child wandered out early, stepping on the Snake while it was sleeping. Startled, the Snake bit the offending limb.

A physician managed to rescue the child, but the leg suffered permanent damage from the bite. Seeing the child permanently marred, the Farmer endeavored to end the Snake with a blade; however, a slice of its tail was all that was managed as the Snake escaped.

Soon the rats returned, and the Farmer sought truce with a bundle of bread and honey. The Snake hissed back, refusing, “For as long as your child’s gait falters, my tail will never grow back.”
