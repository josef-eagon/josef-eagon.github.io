The Donkey and the Wolf

A Donkey, having stepped on a sharp thorn, collapsed to the ground. After some time spent suffering, the Donkey noticed a passing Wolf, and fearing the end was upon him called out, "O great wolf, my last breath is nearly upon me, yet fortune has brought you to me. Spare me the scavengers’ fate—pull this thorn from my hoof, so I may pass to Hades without pain."

"A favor asked, which I shall grant," the Wolf replied, and proceeded to pull the thorn from the inflamed hoof with his teeth. Yet the Donkey, now free from suffering, lashed out with a kick to the wide-mouthed Wolf and fled.

The Wolf, now left with a bruised nose and cracked teeth, said to himself, "Alas, now I suffer; for I took upon the robes of a doctor to heal the lame, when my skills are that of a butcher."

oho! it seems that some predators can be tricked even by simple foes. the most cunning hunters do not fall for simple flattery.
