Hermes and the Sculptor

One day Hermes grew curious to understand how he was regarded by the people, so he set off to find a sculptor's workshop. Disguised as an ordinary man, he found a master Sculptor, with many ornate and beautiful pieces dedicated to the gods.

Seeing a statue of Zeus, he inquired the value of such work. The Sculptor explained the price, and Hermes asked the cost of a statue of Hera, which price was slightly more.

Hermes, beholding a statue of himself, and thinking that he is the messenger of the gods and patron of artisans, he must be well loved, so his statue must be regarded and valued the most of all. The Sculptor announced, "My good fellow, you are a man of excellent taste, so if you buy the other two, I will give you a third one for free."

oho! Perhaps it is best not to assume the answer to a question before asking it. Maybe the sculptor was just trying to pay his bills.
