The Goat and the Donkey

A Goat was envious that a Donkey received an extra meal each day, and decided to deceive the Donkey under the guise of counsel.

He told the Donkey, “You work so hard carrying heavy loads, running the mill, and traveling long distances, yet the Man adds more to your back, whips you, and pushes you further each day.” The Goat suggested that the Donkey fall into a ditch and feign injury, so he might get a chance for rest.

The Donkey, trusting the Goat, agreed to the scheme; however, feign proved folly, for the Donkey stumbled and suffered serious injury.

The Man was concerned for the Donkey which he relied on for so much, and called to a physician who recommended an elixir made from the lungs of a goat. “As luck would have it,” the Man said, “I already have a goat.”
